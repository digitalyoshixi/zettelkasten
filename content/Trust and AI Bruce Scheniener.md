A talk at [[Toronto Area Security Klatch|TASK]] by [[Bruce Schneier]].
# Notes
- Published a book about AI democracy, its optimistic, there are good things happening with AI and democracy
- AI in legislating, government, and assistance
- To trust AI is a barrier
- 4 basic arguments
	- Two kinds of trust (interpersonal, social)
		- Social trust: Reliability, predictability in society
	- AI will continue to be used for confusion
	- AI is a security problem, we as security professionals are responsible for solving this
	- Results from regulatory problems, its the government's role to instill trust in society
- Trust:
	- 'I am trusting a friend', less about specific actions, but more about overall trends ([[Interpersonal Trust]])
	- 'I trust this uber driver', never met them, but we trust them through [[Social Trust]]
- Morals and reputation are person-to-person, underpin [[Interpersonal Trust]]
- Laws, and tech are systems that tell us to act trustworthy, basis of [[Social Trust]]
- Taxi driver used to be one of the most dangerous professions, but Uber changed that, and the rules of the tech ensure that there is safety. Payments are handled by a middlemen, and we only compete for ratings
- Social trust scales better. Without social trust, only my friends can drive my around, nowadays, anybody can
- We use the terms interchangably, so we tend to think of corporation likes friends, the terms and the laws make it an easy mistake to make, we use the same grammar, we imagine we have a relationship with the brand
- Organizations profit when we think of them as friends
- Government, corporations and AI are machines more powerful than its components
- Our fears of AI's are the repressed fears we have of capitalism
