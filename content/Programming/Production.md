The rules that make up [[Programming/Grammar]]. They define [[Non-terminal]].
# Definition
A production rule maps a [[Non-terminal]] to a [[Non-terminal]] or [[Terminal]] statement.
- With $A_{i} \to \gamma_{i}$ as a [[Production|Production Rule]]
- Production defined as: $\alpha \Rightarrow_{G} \beta \Longleftrightarrow \alpha=\alpha_{1}A\alpha_{2}, \beta = \alpha_{1}\gamma\alpha_{2}$
# Examples
Some production rules could look like:
- 'A sentence is a noun phrase followed by a verb phrase'
- 'A verb phrase is a verb or a verb followed by a noun phrase'
