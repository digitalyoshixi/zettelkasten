1. Simulated people would be conscious
2. There is a non-trivial number of civilizations that reach the post-human stage
3. A non-trivial proportion of post-human civilizations are simulations
# Mathematical Backing
With: 
- `p` as the percentage of civilizations that reach a [[Limits of Computation|Post-human]] stage
- `w` as the average number of simulated worlds created by each [[Limits of Computation|Posthuman Simulation]]
- `sim-pop` average number of people per simulated world
- `base-pop` average number of people per non-simulated world
Note, the pecentage of people simulated as opposed to non-simulated is:
$$\frac{\text{\# of sims}}{\text{(\# of sims) + (\# of non-sims)}}$$
This is equivalent to:
$$\frac{\text{p(post-human)}\times \text{w} \times \text{sim-pop}}{(\text{p(post-human)}\times \text{w}\times \text{sim-pop})+\text{base-pop}}$$
