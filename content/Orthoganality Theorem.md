Two non-zero vectors y and v for a right angle if and only if $\langle u, v \rangle = 0$
If they are right angle, we can say they are orthoganal.

# Proof
### Proving $\implies$
Suppose $\theta = \frac{\pi}{2}$
Then, this gives $\langle u, v \rangle = \cos(\frac{\pi}{2}) \mid \mid u \mid \mid v \mid \mid = 0$
### Proving $\Longleftarrow$
Suppose $\langle u, v \rangle = 0$
Then, we get:
$0 = \langle u, v \rangle = \cos(\theta) \mid \mid u \mid \mid \mid \mid v\mid \mid$
Note that $\mid \mid u \mid \mid \mid \mid v \mid \mid \neq 0$
Therefore, $\cos(\theta) = 0$ we get that $\theta = \frac{\pi}{2}$
$$\square$$$$
