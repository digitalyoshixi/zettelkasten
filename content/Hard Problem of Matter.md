*Physics never tells us how matter is in them selves, independently of other things*
The question as to how to explain matter independently without referring to its structure and function.
# Argument
```standardform
P1: All our knowledge about physical objects concerns their structure and function
P2: There must be more to physical objects than just their structure and function
-- Conj --
C: There must be some aspects of physical objects that we aren't in a position to know about
```
# Responses
### Matter is a solid particle
- But, solidity is a functional property that describes how it reacts with other particles
- Therefore, we can conclude that any description of reality in terms of structure and reality is incomplete, because there has to be something underneath that has that structure and function
