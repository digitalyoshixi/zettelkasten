# 802.11b (WI-FI 1)
- Uses [[Frequency Bands|2.4 GHz]]
- Average throughput of 4-6Mbps
- Max throughput of 11Mbps
- Max range of 300ft
# 802.11a (WI-FI 2)
- Uses [[Frequency Bands|5 GHz]]
- Average throughput of 25 Mbps
- Max throughput of 54 Mbps
- Max range of 150ft
# 802.11g (WI-FI 3)
- Uses [[Frequency Bands|2.4 GHz]]
- Average throughput of 25 Mbps
- Max throughput of 54 Mbps
- Max range of 300ft
- Backwards compatible with 802.11b
# 802.11n (WI-FI 4)
- [[Access Point|WAP]] must use 4 antennae to allow for [[Multiple In Multiple Out|MIMO]]
- Supports dual band of [[Frequency Bands|2.4 GHz]] or [[Frequency Bands|5 GHz]]
- 40 MHz channel width 
- Average throughput of 100 Mbps
- Max throughput of 600 Mbps
# 802.11ac (WI-FI 5)
- Allows for 8 [[Multiple User Multiple In Multiple Out|MU-MIMO]] streams
- Supports up to tri-band [[Frequency Bands|5 GHz]]
- Max throughput of 6.933 Gbps
# 802.11ax (WI-FI 6 / WI-FI 6e)
- Allows for 8 [[Multiple User Multiple In Multiple Out|MU-MIMO]] streams.
- Implements [[Orthogonal Frequency-Division Multiple Access|OFDMA]]
- Uses [[Frequency Bands|6 GHz]], [[Frequency Bands|5 GHz]] or [[Frequency Bands|2.4 GHz]]-
- 20, 40, 80, 160 MHz channel widths
- Single channel is 1.2GB/s
- Max throughput of 9.608 Gbps
