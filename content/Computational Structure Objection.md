A counter-argument to the [[Chinese Room]] argument.
- Searle assumes that the computational structure of the Chinese room is indistinguishable from the computational structure of an actual Chinese speaker.
- The brain state of a Chinese speaker does not correspond one to one of a dictionary
- Thus, the structure of the chinese room does not model the same computational structure of an actual chinese speaker
