# Double Integral Version
- Suppose $R$ is a [[Domain|Region]] in $\mathbb{R}^{2}$
- Suppose $f : R \to \mathbb{R}$ is a non-negative function with $f(x,y) \geq 0$
- The volume above $R$ and below $f$ is:
$$
\int \int_{R} f(x,y) dA = \int \int_{R} f(x,y) dxdy
$$
Note that:
- $dA$ is an [[Area Element]] of $dxdy$ and $dydx$
