# Though Experiment
- Father has a genetic blindness issue
- Daughter and father ask doctor to screen daughter if she has the same genetic problem
- Doctor finds that daughter and father are not related
- Two options are: 
	1. Tell daughter and father they are not related + has no illness
	2. Tell daughter that she does not have genetic illness
# Arguments
### Arguments For 1
- Its the doctor's responsibility to tell them before things can get worse
- The daughter would still believe she is genetically related, and could be misinformed of her other cases
- Not saying feels like lying by omission
- Could have legal implications with [[Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act|HIPPA]]
- It is the duty of the doctor to provide them any information that could cause them further problems
### Arguments For 2
- It would be devestating for their family situtation, so we shouldn't cause that harm to people. The harms outweigh the benefits
