A paradox about [[Conditional Statement|Implication]], wherein an arbitrary claim $F$ can be proven by the existance of a sentence $C$ that says itself $C \implies P$.
- $C$
- $C \implies P$
- $P$ ([[Conditional Statement|Implication]])
- $C\implies P$
- $C$
