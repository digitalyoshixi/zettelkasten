### Step 1. Draw the angles out
You have both angles. Draw their general direction.![[Equivalent Ratio Problem Solving Procedure-20231028172414559.webp|414]]
### Step 2. Determine whether to Add or Subtract
There are 2 cases that can happen when you draw them.
##### Case A - Angles same Quadrant
Both angles are in the same quadrant and they both add up to $90^o$
![[Equivalent Ratio Problem Solving Procedure-20231028172619273.webp|351]]
In this case, you would use general [[Co-Function Identities]]
So, $\frac{\pi}{2} - \theta_{1} = \theta_{2}$

![[Equivalent Ratio Problem Solving Procedure-20231028173839369.webp|281]]
##### Case B - Angles different quadrants
Angles are in seperate quadrants. but there is a $90^o$ disparity between them.
![[Equivalent Ratio Problem Solving Procedure-20231028173121146.webp|397]]
The disparity is $90^o$ So you know the other one if you add/subtract $\frac{\pi}{2}$
