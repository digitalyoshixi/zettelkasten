This is the family of [[Antiderivative]] funcitons $F$ of a [[Continuity|Continuous]] function $f$ s.t $\forall x \in I, F'(x) = f(x)$
# Definition
1. Suppose $f$ is [[Continuity|Continuous]] on its domain
2. The indefinite integral is the family of [[Antiderivative|Antiderivatives]] of $f$
# Form
$\int f(x) dx = F(x) + C$
Where:
- $F(x)$ is the [[Antiderivative]] of $f(x)$
- $C$ is  any constant
# Properties
1. If $f(x),g(x)$ are [[Continuity|Continuous]] functions on their domains, then $\int(f(x) + g(x)) dx = \int f(x)dx + \int g(x)dx$
2. $\forall c \in \mathbb{R}, \int cf(x) dx = c\int f(x) dx$
