A mathematical claim that is the starting point for a argument.
When critiquing a [[Mathematical Argument]], you cannot critique the premises, as you assume them to be true all the time (unless you want a [[Metaphysics|Metaphysical]] argument)
Example:
- It will either rain or snow tomorrow
- It will not snow

# Premise Strength
### Weak Premise
- One that assumes at a minimum
### Strong Premise
- One premise that assumes a lot
