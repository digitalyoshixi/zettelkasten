# Richter Scale
The magnitude of earthquake is determined from the logarithm of the amplitude of waves recorded by seismographs
A seismograph produces a seismograph which gets recorded on a ticker tape. it looks like this:
![[Logarithm Scale-20231211144247659.webp|320]]
Scientists use these squiggles to get a single magnitude for the earthquake
##### Magnitude Chart
![[The Richter Scale-20231211224033541.webp]]
Each level is 10x more powerful than the previous level
Magnitude of 3-> Magnitude of 4 is 10x more powerful.
Magnitude 3 -> 5 is 100x more powerful
##### Richter Formula
M = $\log(\frac{I}{I_{o}})$
$M_{1}-M_{2}\log(\frac{I_{1}}{I_{2}})$
$\frac{I_{1}}{I_{2}}=10^{M_{1}-M_{2}}$
##### Example Question
A earthquake of 7.5 magnitude is 375 times as intense as a minor earthquake. find the magnitude of minor earthquake.
$\frac{I_{1}}{I_{2}}=10^{M_{1}-M_{2}}$
$\frac{375I_{2}}{I_{2}}=10^{7.5-M_{2}}$
$375=10^{7.5-M_{2}}$
$M_{2}=7.5-\log375$
= 4.92
