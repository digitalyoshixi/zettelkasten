# Example
1. With points $\{ (1,2), (2,2), (3,4) \}$
2. We get $$A = \left[\begin{array}{cc} 
1 & 1\\
1 & 2\\
1 & 3\\
\end{array}\right]$$
3. We get $$x = \left[\begin{array}{cc} 
a \\ 
b
\end{array}\right]$$
4. We get $$c = \left[\begin{array}{cc} 
2 \\
2 \\
4
\end{array}\right]$$
5. Then, compute $(A^{T}A)x = (A^{T}c)$
6. 
$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 
1 & 1 & 1\\
1 & 2 & 3\\
\end{array}\right]
\left[\begin{array}{cc} 
1&1\\
1 & 2\\
1 & 3\\
\end{array}\right]
\left[\begin{array}{cc} 
a\\
b\\
\end{array}\right]
= 
\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 
1 & 1 & 1\\
1 & 2 & 3\\
\end{array}\right]
\left[\begin{array}{cc} 
2\\
2 \\
4 \\
\end{array}\right]
$$
7. Compute later, and we get to REF and later $x = (\frac{2}{3}, 1)$
8. So, line of best fit is $y = x + \frac{2}{3}$
