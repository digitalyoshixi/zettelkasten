# Finding Bounds
In proving $\lim_{ x \to c }f(x)=L$, $\delta$ when trying to bound $\delta < D$ for some constant $D$, and $f(x)$ has an asymptote at $x = x_{0}$ then $D = |c-x_{0}|$.
### Examples
$\lim_{ x \to 1 }\frac{1}{x^2-2}$
- $c = 1$ (the x at the point of interest)
- $x_{0} = \sqrt{ 2 }$ (the x at the asymptote)
Then $D = |1-\sqrt{ 2 }|=\sqrt{2 }-1$
