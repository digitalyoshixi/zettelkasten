# Properties
### Multiplicative
Multiplying by a positive integer preserves the inequality
$$(a\leq b) \wedge (c>0) \implies ac\leq bc$$
Multiplying by a negative integer triggers [[Negative Flip]]
$$(a<b) \wedge (c<0) \implies ac>bc$$
### Multiplicative Inverse
If both are positive, you can take the reciprocal and then flip the inequality
$$0<a<b \implies \frac{1}{a} > \frac{1}{b}$$
If both are negative, you can take the reciprocal and then flip inequality
$$a<b<0\implies \frac{1}{a}> \frac{1}{b}$$
### Additive
Adding a number to both sides preserves the inequality
$$a<b \implies a+c < b+c$$

$$(a\leq b) \wedge(c\leq d)\implies a+c\leq b+d$$
Two different inequalities, combined will be another inequality
