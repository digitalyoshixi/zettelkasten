$\log_{b}(x^n)=n\log_{b}x$
We are proving
$y=\log_{b}(x)$
Bring the moon(the exponent) down to earth(regular coefficient)
# Proof
$\log_{b}(x^n)=?$
1. Write the equation in exponential form
$b^y=x$
2. Raise both sides to the exponent n
$b^{y^n}=x^n$
3. Apply the power law of exponents
$b^{yn}=x^n$
4. Rewrite in the logarithmic form
$\log_{b}(x^n)=ny$
5. Substitute $y=\log_{b}x$ to eliminate y
$n\log_{b}=x$
We used to have an exponent. But it is gone.
