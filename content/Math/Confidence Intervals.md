Range of estimates at an unknown parameter. Computer at a designated [[Confidence Level]]
### Calculating Confidence Intervals from [[Confidence Level]]
$\overline x-z_{\frac{a}{2}}(\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}})$ < $\mu$ < $\overline x+z_{\frac{a}{2}}(\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}})$ 

### Calculating Confidence Intervals from [[Margin of Error]] 
Intervals = Confidence level $\pm$ margin of error
### Calculative Confidence Intervals from Proportion
**Proportion is p in a binomial distribution**
$\hat{p}$

### Limits - You know what these mean
**Lower limit:** The lowest value in the confidence interval
**Upper limit:** The highest value in the confidence interval

### Two Interval Overlap
If there is overlap between 2 confidence intervals linked to 2 seperate statistics, you are unable to tell which statistic is better than the other
