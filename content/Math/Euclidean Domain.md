This is a [[Integral Domain]] with a divisor algorithm. That is:
# Formal Definition
- With $\mathcal{D}$ as an [[Integral Domain]]
- $\mathcal{D}$ is a euclidean domain if $\forall a,b \in \mathcal{D}$, $\exists$ [[Norm Function|Norm]] on $\mathcal{D}$ $N : \mathcal{D} \to \mathbb{Z}^{+}$ with:
	- $a = qb + r$
	- $r = 0$ or $N(r) < N(b)$
