Assume $P$ and $\neg Q$. End up with a contradiction. This would show that the inditial statement was false and prove $P \implies Q$
Or
Assume $\neg P$, then show $\neg P$ leads to a [[Contradiction]]. This means that $P$ must be true.
Always start the proof with: **by contradiction, suppose**
# Contradictions
You would contradict one of the premises or the conclusion
