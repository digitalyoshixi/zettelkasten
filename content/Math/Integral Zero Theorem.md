The worse version of [[Rational Root Theorem]].
Only works if you have a leading coefficient that is 1.
## Utilization
Find all factors of the leading coefficient \* 6
So, for example, with the function: `f(x) = x^3 - 4x^2 + x + 6`. we want to find the factors of 1 \* 6.
The factors are: +-1, +-2, +-3, +-4.
Use the [[Factor Theorem]] with every one of these factors and check if they are zero to determine the factors.
### Testing the factors
f(1) = 4
f(-1) = 0!! Its factor!
f(2) = 0!!
f(-2) = -20
f(3) = 0!!
f(-3) = -60
f(6) = 84
f(-6) = -360
