# Definition
- With $x, y \in \mathbb{R}^{2}$
- Let $f(x,y) = \sqrt{  \sum_{i=1}^{2}(x_{i} - y_{i})^{2} }$
# Proof of Metric
1. $(x_{i} - y_{i})^{2} \geq 0, \forall i$
2. Then, $\sum_{i=1}^{2} \geq 0, \forall i$
3. Then, $\sqrt{ \sum_{i=1}^{2}(x_{i} - y_{i}) }$ is defined $\forall x,y \in \mathbb{R}^{2}$
4. As domain is positive, $f(x,y) > 0$
