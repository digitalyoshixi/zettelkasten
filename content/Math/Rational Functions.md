# Rational Functions
A function in the form of f(x) = P(x)/Q(x). 
### Denominator stipulations
- Q(x) $\neq 0$
- Q(x) $\neq$ constant. Because dividing P(x) by a constant results in a single polynomial. 
	eg. $\frac{2x^2+4x-8}{2}$ = $x^2+2x-4$
### [[Asymptote]]
Values of x or y which the **INFINITIES** of the graph will never approach.
**INFINITIES** because, [[Cross Over Points]] exist
##### [[Asymptote#Vertical Asymptote|Vertical Asymptote]] (x=?)
When denominator equals 0.
##### [[Asymptote#Horizontal Asymptote|Horizontal Asymptote]] (y=?)
with a rational function in standard form:
$\frac{ax^m+\dots}{bx^n+\dots}$
if m < n, **y = 0**
if m > n, **NO HA**. You have a oblique or parabolic depending on the difference in degree
if m = n, y = a/b
##### [[Oblique Asymptotes]] (y=ax^?+bx^?+cx^?....)
Occurs when the numerator has a higher degree than the denominator.
It is the quotient of numerator/denominator. Find it through [[Long Division Factoring|Long division]] 
You can use [[Test Points]] to determine how to graph an oblique asymptote
### [[Hole]]
A special Asymptote of a coordinate that the graph will never reach.
Occurs when you have a rational function where the numerator and denominator cancel out.
### [[Cross Over Points]]
Points which cross the H.A or O.A
To get this, simply make an equation setting the function equal to the H.A/O.A, then solve.
