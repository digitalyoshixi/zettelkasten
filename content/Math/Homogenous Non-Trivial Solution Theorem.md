If $n < k$, then every [[Homogenous Systems]] with $k$ variables and $n$ equations has a non-trivial solution.

# Proof
1. Suppose $n < k$
2. Reduce the system of [[Echelon Form]]
3. There will be at most $n$ leading $1$s in the system.
4. Then, by defn of free variable, there will exists at least $(k-n)\geq 1$ free variables
5. Therefore, there will be infinitely many solns
6. Therefore, there will be a soln $\overrightarrow{x} \neq \overrightarrow{0}$
