A [[Continuous Probability Distributions]] $\mathcal{N}(0,1)$
- If $X \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^{2}) \implies Z = \frac{X-\mu}{\sigma^{2}} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$
Has:
- average($\mu|\overline x$) = 0
- standard deviation $\sigma$ = 1
