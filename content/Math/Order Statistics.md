# Definition
- For [[Random Variable]] $X_{1},\dots,X_{n}$
- The $k-th$ order statistic $(X_{(k)})$ is the $k$-th smallest variable
	- $X_{(1)} = min(X_{1},\dots,X_{n})$
	- $X_{(1)} \leq X_{(2)} \leq \dots \leq X_{(n-1)} \leq X_{(n)}$
	- $X_{(n)} = max(X_{1},\dots,X_{n})$

Easiest to find when a [[Cumulative Distribution Function|CDF]] is known, or the [[Random Variable|RV]] are [[Independent and Identically Distributed Random Variables]]
