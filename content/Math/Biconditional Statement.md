$A \iff B$.
This is equivalent to saying $A \implies B\ \cap B \implies A$.
In other words, both statements must **prove eachother**. They are necessary and sufficient conditions for each other. 
If both statements are true.
# Bi-conditional Anatomy
$$P\Longleftrightarrow Q$$
$P \Leftarrow Q$ (if)
$P \implies Q$ (only if)
# Equivalent Logical Statement
$$P \Longleftrightarrow Q \equiv (P \wedge Q) \vee (\neg P \wedge \neg Q)$$

# Bi-conditional Negation
For the statement: $P \leftrightarrow Q$, the logical negation is:
$(P \to \neg Q) \vee(\neg A \Rightarrow Q)$
