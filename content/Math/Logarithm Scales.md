# Practical Logarithm Use Cases
### Shrinking Ranges
Log is very good for graphing phenomena that take on a very wide range of values
For example, if we have a table like such that we wanted to graph:

| Animal   | Rat | Cat | Wolf | Horse | Elephant | Whale |
| -------- | --- | --- | ---- | ----- | -------- | ----- |
| Mass(kg) | 0.2 | 4   | 80   | 300   | 5400     | 7000  |
Graphing this out would be horrendous. It would maybe look something like:
![[Logarithm Scales-20231211163454942.webp|184]]
*most of the values are near the bottom*
If we log all masses though,

| Animal   | Rat | Cat | Wolf | Horse | Elephant | Whale |
| -------- | --- | --- | ---- | ----- | -------- | ----- |
| log(mass) | -0.7 | 1.6   | 1.9   | 2.477   | 3.732     | 3.845  |
![[Logarithm Scales-20231211163757798.webp|320]]
We dont even need log paper to graph this out, just get a simple grid paper!
