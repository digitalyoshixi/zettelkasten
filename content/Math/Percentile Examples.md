# Example
The list shows marks for 25 students on a test out of 40.
```
31 28 28 30 20
25 38 40 26 28
15 21 28 36 25
16 21 34 37 30
23 24 36 32 25
```
a) calculate the 80th percentile
b) what percentile is a mark of 25?
c) what percentile is a mark of 40?

### Solution
a) calculate the rank first. We want to know what rank gets us that 80th percentile mark.
$\frac{80(25+1)}{100}$
= 20.8
We round down.
= 20
The 20th highest number is the basis for our 80% percentile.
34 is the 80th percentile

b) data point is 25. there are 7 marks lower than 25. and 3 marks equal to 25.
100(7+0.5(3))/25 = 34
25 is within 34% percentile

c) data point is 40. 24 marks lower than 40. 1 mark equal to 40
100(24+0.5)/25 = 98
40 is within the 98% percentile
