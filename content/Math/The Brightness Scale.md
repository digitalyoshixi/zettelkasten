# Brightness Scale
Brightness of stars between 2 magnitudes
The closer a star is the brighter it is... https://openstax.org/books/astronomy/pages/17-1-the-brightness-of-stars
THE BRIGHTER A STAR IS THE SOONER IT WILL DIE
### Brightness Formula
$m1−m2=2.5\log(\frac{b_{2}}{b_{1}})=2.5(\log b_{1}-\log b_{2})$
$\frac{b_{2}}{b_{1}}=2.5^{m_{1}-m_{2}}$
### Absolute Brightness vs Apparent Brightness
Absolute Brightness is brightness measured from. 32.6 light years(10 parsecs) away.
Apparent Brightness is monkey primal brightness. How our perception sees the brightness of the stars. No consideration to the distance.

m = apparent magnitude
M = absolute magnitude
