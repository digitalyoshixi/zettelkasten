$$\pi:Ax+By+Cz+D=0$$
$$p:(a,b,c)$$
$$d = \frac{|Aa+Bb+Cc+D|}{\sqrt{ A^2+B^2+C^2 }}$$
Alternatively, with [[Normal Vector]] $n = (A,B,C)$
$$d = \frac{n \cdot p}{||n||^{2}}$$
# Example
- Given a plane as $\pi : x+y+z=0$
- Given a vector $v=(1,2,3)$
- We get the [[Normal Vector from Cartesian Equation of Plane]] as $n = (1,1,1)$
- Then, $n \cdot v = 1 + 2 + 3 = 6$
- Then, $||n||^{2} = \sqrt{ 3 }$
- Thus, $d = \frac{6}{\sqrt{ 3 }} = 2 \sqrt{ 3 }$
