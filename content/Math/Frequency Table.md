# Continuous
raw data for grades:
```
76 81 86 55 60 98 55 86 70 75
70 74 70 35 56 79 88 44 51 40
73 88 90 94 99 87 74 77 80 92
```
How we find our range is, we first choose an interval. 10 is a good interval, we can go with 10.
then, total range of data / interval is the # of rows we can have.
the range of our data is between 99 - 35 = 64. 64 is the range
64 / 10 = 6.4. rounded up = 7 rows.

| range     | tally | frequency | relative frequency (experimental probability) |
| --------- | ----- | --------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| 29.5-39.5 | I     | 1         | 1/30                                          |
| 39.5-49.5 | II    | 2         | 2/30                                          |
| 49.5-59.5 | IV    | 4         | 4/30                                          |
| 59.5-69.5 | I     | 1         | 1/30                                          |
| 69.5-79.5 | X     | 10        | 10/30                                         |
| 79.5-89.5 | VII   | 7         | 7/30                                          |
| 89.5-99.5 | V     | 5         | 5/30                                          |
