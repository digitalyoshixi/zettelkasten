Any scalar multiple of vector $\vec{x} \in S$ will also be in $S$
($\vec{x} \in S,k \in R \implies k \vec{x} \in S$)
# Example
[[Subset]] $S = \{ (x,y,z) \in R^3;x\geq 0\}$ is **not** closed under scalar multiplication as:
$(2,2,2) \in S$ but $-5(2,2,2) \not \in S$
