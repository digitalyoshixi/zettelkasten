**Deviation:** how far a score is from the mean.
deviation = score - mean

**Mean Deviation:** the average of all the deviations

**Standard Deviation**: The square root of the mean of the sum of the squares of all the deviations.

Standard deviation is NOT Mean Deviation

# Population vs Sample
### Population
$o=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\mu)^2}{n}}$
$\mu$ is the mean of the population
### Sample
n-1 is the more accurate for samples, thats why its n-1 this time
s = $\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\overline{x})^2}{n-1} }$
$\overline{x}$ is the mean of the sample
# Z-Score
z-score: the number standard deviation a score is from the mean.
### Population
z = $\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}$
### Sample
z = $\frac{x-\overline{x}}{s}$
