A [[System Of Equations]] with $n$ equations in $k$ variables $x_{1},x_{2}\dots ,xk$ in the form:

$$
\empheqlbrace
\begin{alignat*}{4}
   a_{11}x_{1} & {}+{} &  a_{12}x_{2} & {}+\dots+{} & a_{1n}x_{n} & {}={} & b_{1} \\
   a_{21}x_{1} & {}+{} &  a_{22}x_{2} & {}+\dots+{} & a_{2n}x_{n} & {}={} & b_{2} \\
   \dots\\
   a_{j1}x_{1} & {}+{} &  a_{j2}x_{2} & {}+\dots+{} & a_{jn}x_{n} & {}={} & b_{j} \\
\end{alignat*}
$$

Where the entries $a_{jk} \in \mathbb{F}$ and the values $b_{j} \in \mathbb{F}$

The solution to this linear system is are the values that satisfy each equation.
- 1 solution means the system is [[Linear Independence|indep]]
- infinite solutions means the system is [[Linear Dependence|dep]]
