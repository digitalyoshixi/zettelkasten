# Example
With planes:
- $P_{1}:2x-2y+5z+10 = 0$
- $P_{2}:2x+y-4z+7 = 0$
We get:
- $\overrightarrow{n_{1}} = (2,-2,5)$
- $\overrightarrow{n_{2}} = (2,1,-4)$
The line of intersection is the solution for system of equation
![[Intersection Between Two Planes-20250908135750863.webp]]
$\implies 3y-9z-3= 0$
$\implies y = 3z+1$
$\implies 2z+3z+1-4z+7 = 0$
$\implies x = \frac{z}{2}-4$
$\implies$ we get the line relations by setting $z$ as $2t$:
- $x=t-4$
- $y=6t+1$
- $z=2t$, $t \in \mathbb{R}$
$\implies$ we get the line: $(-4, 1, 0) + t(1,6,2)$
