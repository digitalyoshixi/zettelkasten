The property of any field with a conjugate to remain symmetrical from inner products.
$$\langle \beta | \alpha \rangle = \overline{\langle \alpha | \beta \rangle}$$
# Example
1. $\beta = (2+i, 3-i)$, $\alpha = (1-i, 2+i)$
2. $\langle \beta | \alpha \rangle = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \beta_{i} \overline \alpha_{i} = (2+i)(1+i) + (3-i)(2-i) = 8-2i$
3. $\overline{\langle \alpha | \beta \rangle} = \overline{\sum_{i=1}^{n}\alpha_{i}\overline \beta_{i}} = \overline{(1-i)(2-i) + (2+i)(3+i)} = \overline{8+2i} = 8-2i$
