$F(x) = \int_{a}^{x} f(t) \, dt$ is the area accumulation function that gets the area from $[a,x]$ of the integrand $f(x)$.

It adds area as x moves along.
- Note that the function $f(t)$ is integrated with respect to $t$
- Thus, this will track $x$ as it moves along

Often used in [[Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 2|FToC Part 2]]
