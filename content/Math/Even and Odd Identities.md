Derived from the periodic nature of cos, sin and tan functions
# Derivation
### Cosine
![[Even and Odd Identities-20231028015721507.webp|612]]
the cosine function is an even function.
f(x) = f(-x)
Thus, 
$\cos(x) = \cos(-x)$

### Sine
![[Even and Odd Identities-20231028015855084.webp|618]]
the sine function is an odd function. 
f(-x) = -f(x)
Thus,
$\sin(-x) = -\sin(x)$

### Tangent
Believe it or not, tangent function is also an odd function
![[Even and Odd Identities-20231028020017420.webp]]
f(-x) = -f(x)
$\tan(-x) = -\tan(x)$

# Identities
