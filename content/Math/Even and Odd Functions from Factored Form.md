Even and Odd functions are usually determined through [[Standard Form]], that is the best way, however, what if we wanted to save time hm?

## Even Functions

### Double negation
An [[Even and Odd Functions|Even Function]] means that f(x) = f(-x).
for this to be possible, it means that each x intercept has a negative version of it too.
Look at this:
![[Pasted image 20230911172406.png]]
5 and -5.

Thus, each factored polynomial must look something like this:
`f(x) = (x-5)(x+5)`
each x intecept has a copy of itself but negative too
Another example:
`f(x) = (x-7)^2(2x+14)^2`
these are (x-7) and (x+7). negatives of eachother so it is **Even**

### Even powers
Another way you can tell is if each factor has repeated powers.
![[Pasted image 20230911193223.png]]
this is a even graph, the formula is:
`y=(x-2/3)^2(x-5)^2`
This also works because each factor has an even power of 2.
