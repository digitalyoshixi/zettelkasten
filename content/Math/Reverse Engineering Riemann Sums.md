If you want to convert a finished riemann sum into its inputs:
- $a$
- $b$
- $x_{k}$
- $\triangle x = \frac{b-a}{n}$
- $n$
- $f(x)$
- Riemann behavior
# Example
Find $a,b,n, \triangle x, x_{k}$ and riemann behavior of:
$$\sum_{k=1}^{4} (1 + \frac{k}{2})(\frac{1}{2})$$
- $n = 4$ by sigma notation
- Assume $\triangle x = \frac{1}{2}$ for assumption
	- This means: $\frac{1}{2} = \frac{b-a}{n} = \frac{b-a}{4}$
- Assume $x_{k} = a+k \triangle x = 1 + k(\frac{1}{2})$
	- Thus, $a = 1$
	- Thus, $b=3$
- This mirrors the right riemann sum
Done.
