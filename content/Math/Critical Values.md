# Definition
These are points where:
- $c$ is in the domain of $f$
- $f'(c)=0$ or $f'(c)$ is undefined
### Example with Rational Function
$\frac{(x - 2)(x + 1)}{(x+4)(x-3)} \geq 0$
The critical values are when the numerator is zero and when the denominator is zero.
Critical values: x = 2, x = -1, x = -4, x = 3.
# First Derivative Test
If:
- $f(x)$ is has a derivative and is differentiable on $(a,b)$ except possibly at $c$
- If $f'(x)$ is positive on $x \in(a,c)$ and negative on $x \in(c,b)$ Then $f$ has a local max on $x=c$
- If $f'(x)$ is negative on $x \in(a,c)$ and positive on $x \in(c,b)$ Then $f$ has a local min on $x=c$
- If $f'(x)$ is positive for both $x \in(a,c)$ and $x \in(c,b)$ or its negative on $x \in(a,c)$ and $x \in(b,c)$ Then $f$ has no local extremum at x=c
