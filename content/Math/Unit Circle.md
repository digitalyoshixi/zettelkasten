# Step 1. Draw the Lines
There are 16 lines. 3 each quadrant and the 4 quad lines
![[Unit Circle-20231027013221431.webp|460]]
# Step 2. 
Angles is easy to remember.
Firstly. write the quad angles. You know these.
![[Unit Circle-20231210051221015.webp|631]]
Then, write the denominators. It follows a pattern. 3 at top, 3 at bottom, sandwich with 4, and 6. 
![[Unit Circle-20231027014050794.webp|620]]
Now. Remember these numbers: 1,1,1,2,3,5,7,5,4,5,7,11
This is how we arrange them. They are the coefficients of pi and they are the numerators.
![[Unit Circle-20231210051322253.webp]]

# Step 3 - Cosine & Sine
You really only need to remember 3 pairs. (2 really). That is: ($\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2},\frac{1}{2}$)
, ($\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2},\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$) and ($\frac{1}{2},\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$)
![[Unit Circle-20231210051503172.webp]]
Remember CAST rule. This will be how you dictate your next actions. What we do with there 3 remembered pairs.
![[Unit Circle-20231210051624232.webp|652]]![[Unit Circle-20231210051703042.webp|619]]
