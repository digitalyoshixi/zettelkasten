Counting of ordered [[Tuple|Tuples]] from several sets.
# [[Counting Product Rule|Multiplication Rule]]
- With $a$ elements in set $A$
- With $b$ elements in set $B$
- With $c$ elements in set $C$
- $a \times b \times c$ is the total number of ordered pairs $(a_{i}b_{j}c_{k})$
# Intuition
if a process is made of stages with separate choices, the total # of choices is $m*n*p*\dots$ for all choices.
**n** - # of choices in first step
**m** - # of choices in second step
**p** - # of choices in third step
## Hard question example
Athlete has 4 pairs of shoes. How many ways can he pull out 2 unmatched shoes 1 after the other?
