# Theorem
1. If $f,d \in \mathbb{F}[x]$
2. if $d \neq 0$
3. Then, there exists a unique $q, r \in \mathbb{F}[x]$ s.t:
	1. $f = qd +r$
	2. either $r = 0$ or $deg(r) < deg(f)$
4. If the remainder $r = 0$, then we say:
	1. $d$ divides $f$
	2. $f$ is a multiple of $d$
	3. $q$ is the quotient of $f$ and $d$
