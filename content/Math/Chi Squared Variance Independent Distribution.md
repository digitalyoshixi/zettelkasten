For $\frac{(n-1)S^{2}}{\sigma^{2}} \sim \mathcal{X}^{2}_{df=n-1}$
# Proof
- Note that $\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n}(x_{i}- \mu)^2}{\sigma^{2}} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n}(x_{i}-\overline x)^{2}}{\sigma^{2}} + \frac{n(\overline x - \mu)^{2}}{\sigma^{2}}$
# Examples
- With $V[\chi_{(m)}^{2}] = 2m$
- Then, $V[ \frac{(n-1)S^{2}}{\sigma^{2}}]  = 2(n-1)$
- $\implies V[ S^{2}]\frac{(n-1)^{2}}{\sigma^{4}}  = 2(n-1)]$
- $\implies V[S^{2}] = \frac{2 \sigma^{4}}{n-1}$
