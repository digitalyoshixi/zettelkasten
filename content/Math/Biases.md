# Bias Types
### Response Bias
When respondent change their answers to influence results to avoid embarrassment or to give answer they think questioner wants.
Example: raise your hand if you cheated on the test.
**You are intentionally changing your response based on circumstance. However, the interviewer did not intentionally make you feel this way**
![[Biases-20231124172331016.webp|77]]
### Sampling Bias
When a sample does not closely represent the population.
Example: Asking a dry man if they like water.
**When the wrong [[Sampling Techniques]] is being used.**
![[Biases-20231124172654994.webp|159]]
### Measurement Bias
Common bias. When the collection method is such that characteristics are consistently over or under represented. 
Ways they can occur are listed below:
##### Leading
Example: A lot of people do not like math. How would you feel being referred to as a math geek? 
**This wording intentionally guides you to the answer the questioner wants.**
![[Biases-20231124172847848.webp|144]]
##### Loaded Question
You are given a question and 4 answers, none of the answers you would like to pick.
![[Biases-20231124172749214.webp|112]]
### Non-Response Bias 
When the opinions of respondents differ in meaningful ways from those of non-respondents.
Example: Mail in survey asks about drinking habits. 3% respond. Respondents are often radical on the subject. the feeling over or under estimate the feeling of the population
