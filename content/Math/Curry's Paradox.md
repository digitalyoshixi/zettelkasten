A paradox about [[Conditional Statement|Implication]], wherein an arbitrary claim $P$ can be proven by the existence of a sentence $C$ that says itself $C \implies P$.
It is a [[Self-Referential Paradox]]
# Paradox
- This sentence is called $C$, $C \implies P$
- Follows from the mere existence of this sentence that $P$ is true
