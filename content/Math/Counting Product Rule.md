If a procedure can be broken down into a sequence of $m$ tasks where there is:
- $n_{1}$ ways to do the first task
- $n_{2}$ ways to do the second task
- ...
- $m$ ways to do the $m$th task
- Then there are $n_{1} * n_{2} * \dots * m$ ways to do the procedure
