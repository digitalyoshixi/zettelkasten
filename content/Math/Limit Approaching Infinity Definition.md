$\lim_{ x \to \infty }f(x)=L$ where $L \in R$.
Use $\epsilon$ or for y-axis
use $\delta$ or for x-axis
This means that $x$ large $\implies$ $f(x)$ close to L
for $f(x)$ to be close to $L$, then $|f-L| < \epsilon$ (the distance between them) should be close to 0.
For $x$ to be large, $x > \delta$
So, it is :
$$\forall \epsilon >0, \exists N>0$$ such that  $x>\delta \implies |f-L|<\epsilon$
