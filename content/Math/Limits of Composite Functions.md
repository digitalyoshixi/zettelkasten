You evaluate the limit for the inner function first, then you evaluate the limit for the outer function,
# Example $\lim_{ x \to 2^+} f(g(x))$
$\lim_{ x \to 2^+ }f(g(x)) \equiv \lim_{ x \to \lim_{ x \to 2}g(x)}f(x)$
First find $\lim_{ x \to 2^+}g(x) = 2^-$
Since its $2^-$, we will approach $\lim_{ x \to 2^-}f(x) = 1^+$
We can just say its $1$
# DNE Evaluation
If you think that the limit is DNE, be very confident that it is.
Try both one-sided limits and see if they really are different.
