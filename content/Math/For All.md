A [[Quantifier]]
$$\forall$$
For all of a set.
It is applied immediately to the free variable after, $\forall x$,  rather than for the entire statement similar to how $\neg$ is only for a single term after

Make sure you write a comma after you use this in a [[Mathematical Statement]].
Example:
$\forall x \in R, x^2\geq_{0}$
# Proof Writing
$\forall x \in D,P(x)$
First **let** x be an **arbitrary** element of D, then prove $P(x)$ to be true
# Distributive Law
$\forall$ is distributive for [[And]]
$\forall xE(x) \wedge \forall xT(x) \equiv \forall x(E(x) \wedge T(x))$
