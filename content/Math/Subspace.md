A Subspace $W$ is a [[Subset]] of a [[Vector Space]] that has:
- [[Addition]]
- [[Multiplication]]
Which follows the 3 axioms referred to as the [[Subspace Test]]
1. $\vec{0} \in S$
2. S is [[Closed Under Addition]] ($\vec{x},\vec{y} \in W \implies \vec{x}+\vec{y} \in W$ )
3. S is [[Closed Under Scalar Multiplication]] ($\vec{x} \in S,k \in R \implies k \vec{x} \in W$)
Axiom 2 and 3 can be grouped to say that subspaces are [[Linear Combination|Closed Under Linear Combinations]] ($x,y \in W \Longleftrightarrow \forall  c\in F, cx+y\in W$)
# Theorem
1. $W$ is a non-empty [[Subset]] of vector space $V$
2. $W$ is a subspace of $V \Longleftrightarrow \forall x,y\in W$  and $\forall c \in R, cx+y\in W$
# Proving Subspace
1. Prove all 3 axioms for the subset $S$
Or
1. Prove that $S$ is a [[Spanning Set]] for another set $X$ [[Proving Subspace with Spanning Sets]]
