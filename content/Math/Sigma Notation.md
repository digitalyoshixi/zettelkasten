$$\Sigma_{k}^{n}f(x)$$
A condensed way of writing a series
Example: $\Sigma_{k=3}^{12}2k^2 = 2(3)^2+2(4)^2\dots 2(12)^2$
# Definition
$\sum_{k=m}^na_{k} = (a_{m}+a_{m+1}+\dots+a_{n-1}+a_{n})$
# Anatomy
![[Drawing 2025-01-06 13.46.05.excalidraw]]
- $k$ is the index
- $m$ is the non-negative starting index
- $e$ is the non-negative end index
- $e > m$
# Properties of Sigma Notation
### Sum/Diff
$$\sum_{k=1}^{n}(a_{k} + b_{k}) = \sum_{k=1}^{n}(a_{k}) + \sum_{k=1}^{n}(b_{k})$$
### Constant Multiple
$$\sum_{k=1}^{n}c(a_{k}) = c\sum_{k=1}^{n}(a_{k})$$
### Splitting Sum
$$\sum_{k=1}^{n}(a_{k}) = \sum_{k=1}^{l-1}(a_{k}) + \sum_{k=l}^{n}(a_{k})$$
Where $1<l\leq n$
