# Standard Position
The standard position is characterized by this:

*The initial arm lies on the X axis facing right*

An angle is formed when a ray is rotated about a fixed point called the **vertex**
![[Angles in XY Plane-20231025031325281.webp|313]]
The beginning ray is called the **initial arm**
![[Angles in XY Plane-20231025031455866.webp|221]]
The end of the angle is called the **terminal arm**
![[Angles in XY Plane-20231025031613482.webp|319]]
angles are refered to as $\theta$

### Positive or Negative Angles
The angle can be positive or negative depending on what side the terminal arm twists in relation to the initial arm.
**positive angles** are formed from **counter-clockwise** rotations
**negative angles** are formed from **clockwise** rotations

### Coterminal Angles
Angles that share the same terminal arm and the same initial arm.
The easiest way to get these is by adding or subtracting $360^o$
