Spanning sets are equivalent if:
- $span(X) \subset span(Y)$
- $span(Y) \subset span(X)$
This can be done by proving:
- $X \in span(Y)$ (shows $span(X) \subset span(Y)$)
- $Y \in span(X)$ (shows $span(Y) \subset span(X)$)

# Redundant Spans
You can prove equivalent spans because most spans are redundant linear combinations that can be derived from other sets.
