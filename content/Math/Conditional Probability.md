[[Probability]] of a **second event occurring** given that a **first event has already happened**. Requires that one of these events is a [[Dependent Events|Dependent Event]]

This may lead to the [[Sample Space]] for the second event to be smaller or bigger than the sample space of the first event.
$$P(E|F) = \frac{P(E\cap F)}{P(F)}$$
OR
$$P (A \cap B) = P(A|B) * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A)$$
Event probability that are affected by other events is denoted with the Probability: P(A|B). In section: [[Given Probability]]
# Example
1% of people in lost articles realized theyve been punked. 5% of all people are in lost articles.
What are chances that a person at lost articles will realize they've been punked?
P(A) = 5%
P(B|A) = 1%
P(A|B) = 0.2
