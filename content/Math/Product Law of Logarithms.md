The logarithm of a product is equal to the sum of the logarithms of the factors:
# Product Law
$\log_{b}(mn)=\log_{b}m+\log_{b}n$
### Proof
$b^x=m$
$x=\log _bm$
$b^y=n$
$y=\log_{b}n$
Make them products
$b^y*b^x=mn$
$b^{x+y}=mn$
$x+y=\log_{b}(mn)$
$\log_{b}m+lob_{b}n=\log_{b}(mn)$

# [[Power Law]] & Product/Quotient law
Say we had a question like: 
$3\log(x)=\log(2x)$
we must use product law to bring earth back to heaven
$\log(x^3)=\log(2x)$
Then solve like normal
$\log(x^2)-\log(2x)=0$
$\log(\frac{x^2}{2x})=0$
$\log(\frac{x}{2})=0$
$\frac{x}{2}=1$
$x=2$
