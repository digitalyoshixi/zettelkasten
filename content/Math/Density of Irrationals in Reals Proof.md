# Proof
Let $a,b \in R$ be arbitrary.
Note that $a-\pi$ and $b-\pi$ are real numbers.
Then by density of rationals, there exists $r \in Q$ such that $a - \pi < r < b - \pi$
Then $a < r + \pi < b$
note that $r + \pi$ is irrational.
Thus, there exists an irrational number between two real numbers.
$$\square$$
