# Theorem
- For any [[Norm]] $| \cdot | : \mathcal{D} \to \mathbb{R}$ of given [[Integral Domain]]/[[Math/Field]]
- With multiplicative identity $\overrightarrow{ 1}$
- $|\overrightarrow{ 1}| = 1 \in \mathbb{R}$
# Proof
- Let $a$ be the multiplicative inverse of 1
- Then, $1 * a = 1$
- $\implies 1 * |a| = |1|$
- $\implies |1| *| a| = |1|$
- $\implies |1| *| a| - |1|= 0$
- $\implies |1| ( |a| - 1)= 0$
- $\implies |a| = 1$
- As $a$ is the multiplicative inverse of $1$, $|1| = 1$, as $|1| \neq 0$ since $1 \neq 0$
