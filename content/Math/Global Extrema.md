Similar to [[Local Extrema]], except that they are the absolute max or absolute min point in the graph.
There can only be a global minima or a global maxima, there cannot be both.
# Definition
Let $f: U \subset \mathbb{R}^{n} \to \mathbb{R}$ be a function. 
Point $x_{0}$ is a [[Global Extrema|Global Minima]] if $\exists$ [[Open Ball|Neighborhood]] $x_{0} \in V \subset U$ s.t $x \in U \implies f(x_{0}) < f(x)$
Point $x_{0}$ is a [[Global Extrema|Global Maxima]] if $\exists$ [[Open Ball|Neighborhood]] $x_{0} \in V \subset U$ s.t $x \in U \implies f(x_{0}) > f(x)$
### Even VS Odd degree polynomials
Even degree polynomials always have **ONE** minima/maxima
Odd degree polynomials always have **ZERO** minima/maxima, because domain and range are infinite.
