# Theorem
- $A,B \in \mathbb{C}^{m \times n}$
- Then, $\forall X \in C^{n \times 1}, T_{A}T_{B}(x) = T_{AB}(x)$
# Proof
- $T_{A}T_{B}(x) = T_{A}(Bx) = A(Bx) = (AB)(x) = T_{AB}(x)$
- As $T_{AB} \neq T_{BA}$ in general, it follows that compositions are not commutative $\square$
