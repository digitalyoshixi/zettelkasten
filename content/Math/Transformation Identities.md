Identities derived from a $90^o$ shift
# Derivation
If we have 2 graphs. One cosine graph, and another sine graph.
![[Co-Function Identities-20231028014014415.webp|512]]
If the cosine graph was shifted $\frac{\pi}{2}$ degrees to the right, then the 2 graphs would be the same. then it would be an identity
$\cos(x-\frac{\pi}{2}) = \sin(x)$

Additionally, if the sine graph was shifted $\frac{\pi}{2}$ degrees to the left, the 2 graphs would also be the same.
$\sin(x+\frac{\pi}{2}) = \cos(x)$

# Identities
## $\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}-x) = \sin(x)$
## $\sin(\frac{\pi}{2}+x) = \cos(x)$
