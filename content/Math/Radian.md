### Measurement origins
The official definition is: 
*The angle subtended from the centre of the circle which intercepts an arc in equal length to the radius*
How it was originated results in trying to measure an angle, through counting the number of radius-arc-pairs a circle can have. a radius-arc-pair <- *my pseudoterm* is when an angle's arc length subtending it is the same length as the radius.
Example:
![[Radian-20231025034121647.webp|379]]

What they found out was that this was actually a constant. For every circle, no matter the radius, there are exactly $2\pi$ radians

**Dont overcomplicate it**, its just a unit of measurement.
### Formula
a = r \* $\theta$ 
a : arc length
r : radius
$\theta$ : angle in radians

Does this make sense? A radian is when arc length = radius
So radian = arc length / radius = a/r
# [[Radians to Angle]]
### Degree -> Radian
degree angle \* $\frac{\pi}{180}$ 
### Radian -> Degree
radian angle \* $\frac{180}{\pi}$

# [[Angular Velocity]]
Angular velocity of an object is given as the amount of rotation around a central point per unit of time. The amount of ration may be a expression of degrees or radians
### Revolution In degrees / radians
**To degrees:** revolutions \* 360
**To radians:** revolutions \* 2pi
