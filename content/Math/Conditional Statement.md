$P \implies Q$.

If $P$ is true, than $Q$ is true.
$P$ is the **condition/antecedent** and $Q$ is the **result/consequent**
Can also be $Q \Leftarrow P$, 
# Examples
$x$ is odd $\cap$ $y$ is odd $\implies$ $x + y$ is even
This is not true backwards where
$x + y$ is even $\implies$ $x$ is odd $\cap$ $y$ is odd
*an easy [[Proof By Counter Example]] for this is x = 2 and y = 4*
# Proof Writing
$P \implies Q$
First **assume** p is true, then **show** Q is also true.
# [[Logical Equivalence Laws|Contrapositive Law]]
$$P \implies Q \equiv \neg Q \implies \neg P$$
# Negated Conditional Statements
$\neg(P \implies Q) \equiv P \wedge \neg Q$
# English Sayings
1. P implies Q
2. Q, if P
3. P only if Q
4. P is a sufficient condition for Q
5. Q is a necessary condition for P
