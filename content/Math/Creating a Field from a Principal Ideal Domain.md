# Theorem
- Let $(R, +, \times)$ be a [[Principle Ideal Domain|PID]]
- Let $a \in \mathbb{R}$ be a prime element
- Then, $\mathbb{R} \mod a$ forms a field
# Proof
- Let $a \in \mathbb{R}$
- Let $x = qa +r$
- Then, $x \mod a = r$
- As $(R, + , \times)$ is a principal ideal domain
- We must show that $\forall  \in R \mod a$, s.t $d \neq 0, \exists b \in R \mod a$ s.t $d,b = 1$
