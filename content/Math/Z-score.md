Z-score is a way for us to explain how many standard deviations we are from the center(0)

So a z-score of 2 will mean we are 2 standard deviations from the mean
![[Z-score-20231201134321087.webp|414]]

# Z-Score formula
$z=\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}$
$z=\frac{x-\overline x}{s}$
# Standardization
We are able to turn any [[Normal Distribution]] into a [[Standard Normal Distribution]]
For example, this distribution:
![[Z-score-20231201134632574.webp|407]]
$\mu$ = 50
$\sigma$ = 25.81988897

# Z-Score Table
The z-score table is a table to tell us the **percentile rank** of the distribution a z-score means.
![[Z-score-20231201195609955.webp]]
You need to combine a column and row and you get the area (<X) to the left(the percentile rank).
so 3.49 will be the row 3.4 and the column 0.09
