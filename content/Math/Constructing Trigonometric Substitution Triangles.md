The process to create the triangle used for trigonometric substitution from one of 3 given equations:
- $\sqrt{ a^{2}-x^{2} }$
- $\sqrt{ a^{2} +x^{2}}$
- $\sqrt{ x^{2}-a^{2} }$
1. Create a right-angle triangle with $\theta$ as the acute angle
   ![[Constructing Trigonometric Substitution Triangles-20250228151519993.webp]]
2. Given your equation, determine if its the hypoteneuse or if its a side
   ![[Constructing Trigonometric Substitution Triangles-20250228151727276.webp]]
3. Then, you want to choose which sides $a$ and $x$ are. You want to get a relationship s.t $\frac{x}{a}$ is tan, sin or sec
   ![[Constructing Trigonometric Substitution Triangles-20250228151754460.webp]]
