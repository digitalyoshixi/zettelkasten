# Loudness of Sound
##### dB from Intensity
$db = 10log(\frac{I}{I_o})$
$db_{1}-db_{2} = 10log(\frac{I}{I_o})$
##### Intensity from dB
$\frac{I_1}{I_2} = 10^{\frac{(dB_1-dB2)}{10}}$
There is a 10 because its in decibels. if its in bels, then we dont need the 10(s)
$10^{-12}$ is the softest sound
Each successive increment of 10db is 10x louder than the previous. 20db is 10x as loud as 10db
##### Scale

##### Examples
1. How many times more intense is the sound of piano at 67dB to a whisper of 22dB
$10^\frac{67-22}{10}$ = $10^{4.5}$
2. two 80dB phones. do they add up to 160dB? YES? NO?
Convert everything to an intensity. You cant add dB, but you can add intensities
$80 = 10log(\frac{I}{I_{o}})$
$8=log(\frac{I}{I_{o}})$
$10^8=\frac{I}{I_{o}}$
$10^8(I_{o})=I$
$2I = 2(10^8)(I_{o})$
$dB = 10\log(\frac{2(10^8)(I_{o})}{I_{o}})$
$dB = 10\log(\frac{2(10^8)}{1})$
$dB = 83.01$
