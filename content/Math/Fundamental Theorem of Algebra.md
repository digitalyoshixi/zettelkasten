Every complex function will be perfectly factorable.
# Theorem
1. $p(x) \in P_{n}(\mathbb{R})$ is a degree $n$ polynomial with [[Real Number]] coefficients represented as $p(x) = x^{n} + a_{n-1} + \dots + a_{0}$
2. Then, $p(x) = (x-\lambda_{1})^{m_{1}} * \dots (x - \lambda_k)^{m_{k}}$
Where:
- $m_{1}+\dots+m_{k} = n$
- $\lambda_{i} \in \mathbb{C}$
# Alternate Definition
Every $f \in \mathbb{C}[x]$ factors completely. 
In other words, 
- $\exists c_{1},\dots,c_{k} \in \mathbb{C}$ 
- $\exists r_{1},\dots,r_{k} \in \mathbb{Z}$
Such that:
- $f = a(x-c_{1})^{r_{1}}\dots(x-c_{n}^{r_{n}})$
