A common problem in Proof Writing to highlight how easy it is to assume incorrect [[Conjecture]]s.
# Definition
1. On a circle, with $n$ number of points, draw chords connecting each point to every other point.
2. The $n$ points should never be arranged in such a way that creates 3 chords intersecting at a same point
   ![[Moser's Circle Problem-20240829175032243.webp|249]]
3. The number $r$ is the number of triangular regions created by the chords
# Incorrect Assumption
![[Moser's Circle Problem-20240829175659693.webp]]
From a glance, it appears that the pattern grows according to $R = 2^{n-1}$
