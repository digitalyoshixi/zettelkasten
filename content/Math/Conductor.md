The set of polynomials $g$ that allow for the $g(T)$ of a linear transform $T$ to map values of a given $\alpha$ into a given set $W$.
# Definition
- With $V$ as a $\mathbb{F}$ [[Vector Space]]
- With $T \in \mathcal{L}(V)$
- With $W$ as an [[Invariant|Invariant Subspace]] for $T$
- With $\alpha \in V$
- Then, the $T$-conductor of $\alpha$ into $W$ is the set $S_{T}(\alpha ; W) = \{ g \in \mathbb{F}[x] | g(T)\alpha \in W \}$
# Intuition
- Every $g \in S_{\alpha}(\alpha; W)$ will map values of $a$ into $W$
