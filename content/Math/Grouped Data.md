Grouped data is when you are turning an interval into an average of the data. this is used 
# Example 1
Time taken to complete a chess game is as follows:

| time      | 10-15 | 15-20 | 20-25 | 25-30 | 30 - 35 |
| --------- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ------- |
| frequency | 2     | 20    | 18    | 10    | 5        |

To find the tendency, we must first think. I dont know the time values they give me, because they are interval. I should average out the time.
so interval 10->15 = **12.5**
interval 15->20 = **17.5**

so if we summed all the data:
$12.5*2 + 17.5*20 + 22.5*18+27.5*10+32.5*5 = 1217.5$
The average is the sum divided by the total # of data points. 
$avg = \frac{1217.5}{55} = 22.136$
since grouped data is the midpoint of the interval, it gets less accurate as the interval gets larger
