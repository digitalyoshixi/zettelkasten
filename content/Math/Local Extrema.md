# Local Maxima
**Maximas** are points where the surrounding slopes point downwards.
![[Pasted image 20230907203008.png]]
### Loose Definition
If $f''(c)<0$
### Formal Definition
$$\exists \delta>0, 0<|x-c|<\delta \implies f(c)\geq f(x)$$
# Local Minima
**Minimas** are points below their surrounding slopes.
![[Pasted image 20230907202954.png]]
### Loose Definition
If $f''(c)>0$ 
### Formal Definition
$$\exists \delta>0, 0<|x-c|<\delta \implies f(c)\leq f(x)$$

# Finding leading [[degree]] from \# of turning points
The degree of the graph, will always have at max, (**degree-1**) number of turning points.

in our graph above, there are 3 turningo points.
This gives us lots of options, but we know at least that it is **at least a degree 4 or higher graph.**

Pair this with a second, or third method of determining powers like [[Endpoint Behavior]] or [[Finite Differences]] to find out the actual leading [[degree]] of the graph
