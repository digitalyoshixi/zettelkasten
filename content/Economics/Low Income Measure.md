Relative measure of low income.
LIM is a fixed percentage of 50% of median adjusted household income (adjusted in the fact that household needs are taken into account and this need increases as household members increase). 
![[Low Income Measure-20240506201004792.webp]]
A household is considered low income if its after-tax income is less than half of median after-tax income of all households in Canada.

This is the most common measure for international comparisons
