Canada's income earners are divided into quintiles.
The percentage of the country's income and the quintile are arranged in a graph like such:
![[Lorenz Curve-20240506152841676.webp]]
The graph should follow a straight line.
![[Lorenz Curve-20240506153028971.webp]]
If it does not, then it is compared to the straight line, the area is found. This area is passed to some formula and the [[Gini Coefficient]] is computed.

If it is not apparent that Income is proportional to quintile, then Income% is cumulative regarding each population quintile. If you add them all up, you get 100%
