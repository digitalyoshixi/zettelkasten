A small piece about the representation of the entire [[Concrete State]] of a program. 
- Sign of a variable $\{ -,0,+ \}$
- Intervals $[a,b]$
- Congurences (even or odd)
- Octagons (difference between two variables bounded)
- Polyhedra (what linear relations hold between variables)
![[Abstract State-20260621223417751.webp]]
# Properties
- Concretisation: The concrete value is always a subset of the abstract version
- Re-abstracting a value cannot be more precise than the original abstraction
