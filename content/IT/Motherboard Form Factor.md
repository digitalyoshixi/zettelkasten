Motherboard form factor determines the compatibility of [[System Unit|PC Case]] and [[Power Supply Unit]].
# Form Factors
### AT
![[Motherboard Form Factor-20240525183032272.webp|360]]
AT is now obsolete. Was very very large.
It lacked external ports and could only handle a monitor and keyboard
### ATX
![[Motherboard Form Factor-20240525183456230.webp|409]]
ATX improves on AT by having:
- All necessary ports built in
- Better [[Power Supply Unit|PSU]] placement
- Better CPU and RAM placement
- Shorter wires with better shielding
- Capable of handling 4x the clockspeed
- [[Soft Power Button]]
- 2 more power cables
- All support [[Multi-Channel Architecture|Dual-Channel]]
Good for:
- Desktop computers
##### Micro ATX
![[Motherboard Form Factor-20240525184216796.webp|303]]
Uses the same connections as the ATX.
Good for:
- Desktop computers
### ITX
The standard small form factor(SFF) motherboard.
- Fully compatible with ATX cases.
- Draws little power and produces little heat. 
- Best for [[Home Theater PC]]
- Best for [[Embedded Device]]
