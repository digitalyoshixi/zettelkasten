How fast the hard drives rotates determines how fast it can retrieve and store data.
Most common speeds are 5400 RPM and 7200 RPM.
Fastest drives have 10,000 or 15,000 RPM
Faster drives generate a lot more noise and heat. A rise of $5^oC$ may result in 2 years less life expectancy.
