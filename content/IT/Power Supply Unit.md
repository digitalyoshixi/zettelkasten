# PSU
Takes the high voltage [[Alternating Current|AC]] from wall socket and converts it into low voltage [[Direct Current|DC]] that your computer can use.
![[Power Supply Unit-20240625191701416.webp|395]]

# DC/AC Conversion
In US, standard AC comes at 115V.
The rest of the world uses 230V for AC.
Most modern supplies support both these [[VAC Standards]].
Older power supplies had fixed-input switches that required you to set 115V or 230V
![[Power Supply Unit-20240625195542935.webp|195]]
# Installation
1. Take out 4 screws at the back of your case and then secure the PSU in
   ![[Power Supply Unit-20240627145819184.webp|263]]
2. Enable the switch at the back of the PSU to turn it on
   ![[Power Supply Unit-20240627145950960.webp]]
	1. If the switch is not installed, or the leads are not plugged into the motherboard, you can use a metal material to contact the 2 stop and start wires.
	   ![[Power Supply Unit-20240627150441691.webp|261]]
3. If the PSU is making sound, then it means the fan is runninng and the system is fine.
