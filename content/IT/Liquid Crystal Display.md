A display mode that uses a backlight and crystals to generate images.
![[Liquid Crystal Display-20240729165114274.webp]]
1. DC current runs through an [[Inverter]]
2. [[Inverter]] sends AC current runs to [[Light Emitting Diode|LED]] or [[Cold Cathode Fluorescent Lamp|CCFL]] backlight
3. Tiny liquid crystals arranged in a grid act as a shutter to enable light to pass through
4. Tiny transistors change subpixel status on translucent subpixel sheets to provide different colors
   ![[Monitors-20240715202238608.webp|428]]
# Backlight
To create the image, [[Inverter|Inverters]] convert DC power into AC power to be used by the backlight powered by [[Light Emitting Diode|LED]] or [[Cold Cathode Fluorescent Lamp|CCFL]]
### Edge Backlighting
Putting backlight LED's only on the edges of the monitor.
The center's color is a little bit dimmer
### Direct Backlighting
Putting a bank of LEDs across the entire monitor back. Uniform color, but a bit more expensive
