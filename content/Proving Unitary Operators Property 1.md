# Property
$$UU^{*} = I = U^{*}U$$
# Proof
1. $\langle U \alpha | \beta \rangle = \langle U \alpha | I \beta \rangle = \langle U \alpha | U U^{-1} \beta \rangle = \langle \alpha | U^{-1} \beta \rangle$
2. Therefore, $U^{-1} = U^{*}$ are adjoins if existing $\alpha$ is unique
3. Now, we show $U$ [[Preserving Inner Products and Vector Isomorphism|Preserve Inner Product]]
4. $\langle U \alpha| U \beta \rangle = \langle \alpha | U^{*} ( U \beta) \rangle = \langle \alpha | I \beta \rangle = \langle \alpha | \beta\rangle$
