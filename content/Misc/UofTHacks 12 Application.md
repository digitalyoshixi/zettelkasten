# Describe Situation Where You Learned Something New Quickly
I tried hardware development coming in with no prior experience in any past hardware projects. I decided I wanted to build a custom keyboard as I had been seeing many rust and emacs programmers recommend the ergonomic builds. I had to do extensive research into the builds that I wanted to try out, but still ended up purchasing a few parts that were incompatible. The reason why there was a mismatch was because I was combining choices I made between different guides. Hardware development is very different from software development, you cannot easily mix and match solutions into your project without expecting some conflicts. I ended up finding this resource that ended up guiding me through essential theory in keyboard making (https://deskthority.net) which ended up saving me a lot of future headaches and led me to build the corne-GLP keyboard. During this I also sifted through a electrical engineering textbook and plan to build a simple NAND gate adder in the future.
