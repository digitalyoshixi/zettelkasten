# Definition
$Alg(\lambda)$ of an eigenvalue $\lambda$ is the number of times that it occurs as a root of the [[Characteristic Polynomial]] of $T$
# Example
For the matrix
$$
\left[\begin{array}{cccc} 
1 & 1 & 0 & 0\\
0 & 1 & 0 & 0\\
0 & 0 & 2 & 0\\
0 & 0 & 0 & 3\\
\end{array}\right]
$$
We get that the [[Characteristic Polynomial]] is: $(\lambda-1)(\lambda-2)^{2}(\lambda-3)$
- It follows that the eigenvalues are: $\lambda_{1}=1,\lambda_{2}=2,\lambda_{3}=3$
Then, $Alg(1) = 2, Alg(2) = 1, Alg(3) = 1$
