A cryptographic break means simply reducing the number of complexity a [[Brute Force]] attack must take
# Quote
*"Breaking a cipher simply means finding a weakness in the cipher that can be exploited with a complexity less than brute force. Never mind that brute-force might require 2128 encryptions; an attack requiring 2110 encryptions would be considered a break...simply put, a break can just be a certificational weakness: evidence that the cipher does not perform as advertised."* - [[Bruce Schneier]]
