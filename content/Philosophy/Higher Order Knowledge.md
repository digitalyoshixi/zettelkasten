To actually have knowledge, you need to know that you know you have knowledge.

*To know that I have knowledge of calculating the [[Determinant]] of a matrix, I need to know that I do indeed have that knowledge*

Leads into the [[Higher Order Knowledge Paradox]]
# KK Rejection
Most philosophers reject the KK principle.
- It seems plausible that there are things that you know but that you dont know that you know (I know that I have hands, but I don't know that I know I have hands)
