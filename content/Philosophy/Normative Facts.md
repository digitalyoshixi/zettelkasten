A [[Fact]] in relation to a norm or standard.
# Properties
- Value-oriented judgements.
	- Can be about ideals (how things should be)
	- Can be opinions (how you personally see things)
- It cannot be proven or denied
- [[Moral Motivation|Motive Internalism]]. Normative statements innately motivate

Contrasts with [[Positive Facts]]
# Examples
- 'Children should eat vegetables'
- 'You should put your hand in the fire'
- 'It is wrong to commit adultery' (Note this is also a [[Moral Facts|Moral Fact]])
- 'Orange is better than red'
# [[Descriptive Claim]] $\not\Rightarrow$ Normative Claim
There is no way to get a normative claim from a descriptive claim.
You can say descriptive claims like "If you kill my family I will feel bad", but you cannot deduce that "You cannot kill"
