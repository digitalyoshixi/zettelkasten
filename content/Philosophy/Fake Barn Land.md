A counter-example to knowledge comprised of [[No False Premise Knowledge]] and [[Causation Knowledge]].
# Hypothetical
1. You are driving to countryside and point at a barn saying that is a nice looking barn
2. You don;t know that you are in a fake barn land where there are cardboard barns
3. Coincidentally, the object you point to is the single real barn
4. You have a justified true belief
5. You are pointing to a barn
6. Your belief is based only on true premises
7. Your belief seems to be caused by the fact

Most people think this does not amount to knowledge, they say you just got lucky
