Believing in a claim without further evidence and to be committed to the claim even when counter arguments arise.

You can have faith in $X$ if:
- You are willing to take a risk on $X$ without looking for additional evidence
- You are willing to follow through on risky actions even when provided counter-evidence against $X$
Additionally:
- Faith only exists in positive connotations
# Link to risk
The more risk you put, the more faith you have.
# Faith to protect against misleading evidence
Since faith exists despite counter evidence, having some faith is great for allowing you to reach your goals when misleading evidence arises.
