# Experiment
- Allies that liberated concentration camps in starving areas
- People who have been starved for a while, when given access to lots of food would die (heart attack, other issues)
- What is the best way to renourish?
- Ansel Keyes runs a minnesota starvation experiment, works as follows:
	- 36 young men that are objectors to the war ([[Quakers]])
	- For 12 weeks, fed 3200 calories a day
	- For 24 weeks, fed 1560 calories a day
	- For 12 weeks, rehab diet. More carbs, protiens, various different diets
	- For 8 weeks, unrestricted time, evaluation of results
- Subjects allowed to leave at any time
# Results
- 25% weight loss from normal [[Body Mass Index|BMI]]
- Two subjects were dismissed for eating other food
- All subjects experienced psychological effects like:
	- [[Hysteria]]
	- [[Hypochondria]]
	- [[Self Harm]]
	- [[Depression]]
# Response
- Study was not ethical, permanent effects on participants
- They could have studied existing starving person, but less controlled study, might be a tougher ask aswell
- Researchers could have not forseen the effects
- Starving people is just too much harm in a study
- Respondants respond that they would still participate again. Humanity has improved, they knew it wouldnt be easy but they were prepared. No resentment to researchers
