The relationship between [[Patient]] and physician that acknowledges:
- Vulnerability
- Loyalty
# Responsibilities
1. Accept the patient without discrimination
2. Continue to provide services until these services are no longer required
3. Act according to your [[Conscience]]
4. Inform the patient when moral commitments may influence your recommendation of a procedure
5. Communicate information accurately and honestly
6. Recommend evidence-based treatment whenever possible
7. Limit treatment of yourself, or other cases when another physician is not available
8. Provide whatever assistance you can to a person who needs emergency care
9. Ensure any research you do is evaluated both scientifically and ethically
10. Never participate or condone [[Torture]]
11. Empower the patient to make informed decisions with their health
12. Respect decisions of competent patient to accept or reject and recommended assessment
13. Recognize the maturity of a minor's right to consent to treatment or care
14. Accommodate patient with cognitive impairments to participate
15. Respect values and intentions of patients deemed incompetent
16. When intentions of incompetent patient are unknown, act in patient's best interests
17. Respect the patient's reasonable request for a second option
