# Argument
```standardform
P1: To be truly responsible for what you do, you must be truly responsible for the way you are
P2: You can't be truly responsible for the way you are
-- MP --
C: You cant be truly responsible for what you do
```
# Justifications
## Justification for P2
```standardform
P2.1: To be responsible for the way you are, you must have intentionally brought it about that the way you are
P2.2: It is impossible to intentionally bring about that you are the way you are
```
### Justification for P2.2
- You are given your [[DNA]], you are not responsible for having the things you have
- A person cannot bring about their own nature
# Responses
## Denial of P1
Even if your action is determined by other factors, it is still you who acted the way you did because of your strength of will, reasoning, personality
### Strawson's Reponse
Your willpower, reasoning, personality are given to you by something else. You are not ultimately responsible.
